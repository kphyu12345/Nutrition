What is NutriLife?

NutriLife is a website that aims to help users—monitor food consumption, manage calorie intake, and promote healthier choices to sustain a nourishing lifestyle—while guiding them with easy, accessible, and healthy meal ideas and explanations on nutritional value



Carbohydrates

Advantages of Carbohydrate Consumption in Moderate Amounts: provides fibre to the body, helps to prevent diseases, energy for the body, prevents blood clots, an improves the digestive system. Disadvantages of Carbohydrate Consumption in Excess Amounts: high blood pressure, obesity, heart disease, diabetes, stroke, cholesterol


(Dietary) Fibre

Advantages of (Dietary) Fibre Consumption in Moderate Amounts: less chance of constipation, improves bowel health, prevents blood sugar spikes, supports gut health, and may reduce the risks of colon cancer. Disadvantages of (Dietary) Fibre Consumption in Excess Amounts: gas and bloating or constipation, can reduce your body's absorption of certain minerals, including calcium and magnesium, gastrointestinal effects, and abdominal pain.

Protein

Adv of Protein Consumption in Moderate Amounts: proteins are made up of building blocks called amino acids, there are around 20 different amino acids that link together, your body uses amino acids to make new proteins: such as muscle and bone, and other compounds like enzymes and hormones, which can also be used as an energy source.

How to find my carbs intake?
Carbs are measured in grams. On packaged foods, you can find total carb grams on the Nutrition Facts label. You can also check this list or use an app to find grams of carbs in foods and drinks.
For diabetes meal planning, 1 carb serving is about 15 grams of carbs. This isn't always the same as what you think of as a serving of food. For example, most people would count a small baked potato as 1 serving. However, at about 30 grams of carbs, it counts as 2 carb servings.
How to find my protein intake?
There is protein in some, but not all foods. To count protein, you will need to use information from food labels and the food lists. Use labels where possible on packaged foods to calculate protein content. For reference, 1 oz of egg is 6g of protein.
How to find my (Dietary) Fibre intake?
It's most reliable to look at the Nutrition Factor label on the products, but if it's not available, check the fibre counter lists and labels which give the accurate fibre content. For example, kidney beans have 17g per 250ml.
